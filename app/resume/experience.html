Technology Summer Analyst

May 2015 - August 2015

Goldman Sachs, Jersey City, NJ

- My first official internship in US

Summary: Somebody told me that working in a big firm was like being a small component of a rocky. He means that you might be important but not that necessary for the whole launch. I totally agree on this statement in the scale of a firm, but I think as a member of a team (even just a small intern), you will still be very essential. At least I felt I was.

At the very beginning, I tried very hard to understand the exiting business problem, which was their difficult to use realistic trading order to test our surveillance application. Then, I provided and designed the solution - to build a light weight front-end web tool to simulate what a actual trading order entry system can do. The tool can quickly collect the order data and send it to the RESTful service for further validation and to convert to JAVA objects and send it to the surveillance application. When it returns back results, the tool can represent results in an elegant way.

After the approval of my solution, I started with a simple model to make sure the idea worked well and then built out the app with user friendly UI. Then I added more powerful and intelligent functions to make order entry faster and smarter. I went through the whole software development cycle.

The tool greatly reduced team members' time on testing and provided the ability to do complex tests.

Technology: AngularJS, JavaScript, JSON, HTML5/CSS3, Node.JS, REST, Jasmine, IntelliJ, Tomcat

Details:

  • Developed a mock order entry system to interact with a compliance surveillance application.
  • Used AngularJS, Node.js and Jasmine for front-end and RESTful web services as middle layer.
  • Reduced data entry time and enhanced workflow by implementing intelligent form auto-filling and data collection.
  • Provided the ability to perform complex and realistic tests to meet functional and regression testing needs.

Thoughts: My first impression in Goldman Sachs is that this financial firm invested a lot in its technology. This internship helped me improve significantly on my programming, problem solving, fast learning, communication, teamwork, design skills. I am grateful to everyone who helped me throughout the summer.

Research Assistant

May 2014 - December 2014

ECE Department, Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, IL

- Faster than Real-Time (FTRT) Power System Dynamics Simulation

Summary: Our research was separated into two phases: summer 2014 and fall 2014. We had a group of 3 in the summer. Our task was to build the whole wind turbine model in Simulink and to verify the difference of Simulink result and PSSE result was small enough. But the Generic type 3 is too complicated to fully understand and properly built in MATLAB. The documentation in PSSE is not detailed and even has some errors. But after reading all the articles from internet and many times of failure, we eventually solved most of the miseries.

In the second phase, we continued working on this model, but our task was to build the whole Simulink model into C program to help extend TS3ph Simulator. We finished coding most of the components, but the simulator lacked the adaptor like set up for our wind turbine model.

Technology: C, PETSc Toolkit, Linux Server, Emacs, Git, MATLAB Simulink

Details:

  • Managed a team of five to implement wind turbine models into source code, extended model library of high performance power system simulator.
  • Identified and solved 50 missing or hidden parts in the standard model from PSSE documentation; successfully established Simulink models to match the PSSE simulation by 0.5% comparison offset.
  • Translated differential equations from Laplace to State-Space, reaching the required 10e-13 V/A comparison criteria.

Thoughts: There were pitfalls, disappointment, innovative ideas, many months of effort and the joy of success throughout our research. I learned a lot and realized how much more a team can produce than an individual can produce. Finally, a special thanks to Professor Alexander Flueck. He gave me this valuable opportunity and helped me a lot in the rest of my academic years and the start of my career.
